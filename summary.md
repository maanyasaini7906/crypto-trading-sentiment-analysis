# Summary

This project explores the relationship between market sentiment and trader performance.

The analysis reveals that traders tend to perform better during bullish (Greed) periods, with higher profitability and win rates. In contrast, bearish (Fear) conditions lead to lower returns and more volatile behavior.

Behavioral patterns indicate that traders increase position sizes during Fear, suggesting emotional or panic-driven trading.

Based on these findings, two key strategies are recommended:
1. Increase participation during Greed while managing risk
2. Reduce exposure during Fear to avoid losses

Overall, market sentiment significantly influences trading outcomes and behavior.
